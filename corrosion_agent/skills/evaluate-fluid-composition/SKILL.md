---

## Instructions

1. **Evaluation of Corrosive Agents:**
* Analyze the concentrations of Carbon Dioxide ($CO_2$), Hydrogen Sulfide ($H_2S$), and Oxygen ($O_2$).
* Determine the impact according to the manual's limits: elevated $CO_2$ concentrations (> 2%) or the presence of $H_2S$ in humid environments drastically increase the probability of internal corrosion (pitting or generalized corrosion).


2. **Identification of Missing Data:**
* Verify if the values come from recent chromatographic analyses or if the "70% Rule" was applied due to lack of information.
* In case of uncertainty, the mandatory recommendation is to perform a sampling and laboratory analysis of the fluid.


3. **Generation of Recommendations Based on the Manual:**
* **High $CO_2/H_2S$ Presence:** Recommend the implementation or adjustment of corrosion inhibitor injection rates.
* **Presence of Free Water:** If the fluid composition suggests the possibility of water settling, recommend frequent internal cleaning (pigging) programs to remove stagnant electrolytes.
* **Bacterial Control:** If biogenic $H_2S$ is detected, suggest treatments with biocides.


4. **Monitoring and Mitigation:**
* Suggest the installation or review of corrosion coupons and electrical resistance probes at points where the fluid composition is most aggressive.



## Examples

**Example 1: Gas with high CO2 content**

* **Analysis:** The fluid has a $CO_2$ concentration of 3.5% with residual moisture presence.
* **Recommendation:** The composition is highly aggressive. It is recommended to ensure compliance with the dew point to avoid liquid water and optimize the film-type corrosion inhibitor injection program, verifying its efficiency through coupons.

**Example 2: Uncertainty in composition**

* **Analysis:** No updated fluid chromatographies have been available in the last year (Score assigned by uncertainty: 70% of the maximum).
* **Recommendation:** Perform a complete chemical analysis of the transported fluid immediately. The lack of data prevents validating whether the current mitigation scheme is sufficient for the real chemical conditions of the pipeline.
